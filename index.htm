biography

I have always been a problem-solver, someone who thinks about solutions, as well as what to do to bring it to life. While recognizing the end user in software development can lead to more effective user experience, it takes someone to represent the user's needs and make sure their voice is heard in the creative process.

Getting my Masters degree in human-computer interaction and design solidified theory behind toolsets I had been using for years as part of a team developing software. I started my career as a web developer, working with creative and technical team members building out application websites for an online lender. Over time, my role morphed into one where I was the only team member doing user needs analysis, working with development on solutions and testing to get feedback and acceptance with the customer. Since then, I have been an advocate for the user's voice in my roles involving making software and business processes easier to use and less error-prone.

Growing up in southern california I surfed, mountain biked and snowboarded all over california and beyond. Nowadays, when I'm not making pizza or hanging out with my cats, I'm in the woodshop turning old wood into bowls and one of a kind art. During the long summer days, you can find me hiking local trails; such as the one at "Top of the World" in Laguna Beach, CA.
